Newark

ABOUT NEWARK

Founded in 1666 and located in the northeastern part of NJ, Newark has long been the largest city of the state. It greatly expanded during the Industrial Revolution, becoming the commercial and cultural hub of the region.

As per classification, Newark is considered as an urban city. Impressively, the median household income is approximately $52,888 which is significantly lower than NJ, which, by reference, is around $101,133.

Lastly, as for 2022, there are over, 339 people living in Newark. That's 12,646.8 people per square mile!

FACTS

  • Newark is the location of one of the busiest airports in the United States, Newark Liberty International Airpot.

  • The city has the largest museum of the state which features American art, decorative arts, and even a planetarium!

  • On top of that, Newark is home to several institutions of higher education, including Rutgers University and the New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT)
